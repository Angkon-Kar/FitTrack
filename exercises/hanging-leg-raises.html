Hanging Leg Raises

Hanging from a bar, raise legs. Maximum lower ab and hip flexor challenge.

Lower Rectus AbdominisHip FlexorsObliquesForearms
Category Core
Difficulty 🔴 Advanced
Primary Lower Rectus Abdominis, Hip Flexors
Sets · Reps 3 · 8–12
📋 Step-by-Step Form
1
Dead Hang
Hang from bar with overhand grip. Fully relaxed arms. Core slightly pre-engaged.
2
Posterior Pelvic Tilt
Before raising legs, tuck pelvis slightly. This engages abs before hip flexors.
3
Raise Legs
Keep legs straight and raise until parallel to floor or higher. Control the movement.
4
Slow Descent
Lower legs slowly. Do NOT swing. Full descent before next rep.

⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Swinging and using momentum — strict control only
✗Not tucking pelvis — hip flexors dominate instead of abs
✗Bending knees excessively — try to keep legs straight
✅ Pro Tips
→Bent knee raises are the easier progression to start
→L-sit position at top maximizes core contraction
→Chalk or straps when grip fails before abs do
