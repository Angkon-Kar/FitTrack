Glute Bridge

Floor-based glute activation. Essential for athletic performance and lower back health.

GlutesHamstringsCoreLower Back
Category Legs
Difficulty 🟢 Beginner
Primary Glutes, Hamstrings
Sets · Reps 3 · 15–20
📋 Step-by-Step Form
1
Lie on Back
Knees bent 90°, feet flat on floor hip-width apart, arms at sides.
2
Brace and Drive
Press through heels. Drive hips up by squeezing glutes forcefully.
3
Peak Position
Hips fully extended. Shoulders, hips, knees in a straight line. Squeeze hard 2 seconds.
4
Controlled Descent
Lower hips slowly. Lightly touch floor and immediately drive back up.
🌬️ Breathing Pattern
Going Down
Inhale
At Bottom
Exhale squeezing up
Coming Up
Inhale down

⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Hyperextending lower back at top — squeeze glutes not back
✗Feet too far forward — position them under knees
✗Not achieving full hip extension at top
✅ Pro Tips
→Place resistance band above knees to increase glute activation
→Single-leg version is highly effective for strength
→3-second hold at peak dramatically increases difficulty
