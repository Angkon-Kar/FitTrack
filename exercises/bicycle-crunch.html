Bicycle Crunch

EMG studies rate this as one of the most effective ab exercises. Hits rectus AND obliques.

ObliquesRectus AbdominisHip Flexors
Category Core
Difficulty 🟡 Intermediate
Primary Obliques, Rectus Abdominis
Sets · Reps 3 · 20 total
📋 Step-by-Step Form
1
Lie on Back
Hands lightly behind head. Lower back pressed into floor. Knees at 90°, feet off floor.
2
Crunch and Rotate
Bring right elbow toward left knee simultaneously. Extend right leg straight.
3
Switch Sides
Bring left elbow toward right knee. Extend left leg. This pedaling motion = one rep each side.
4
Continuous Motion
Controlled cycling rhythm. Do not rush — slow is harder and significantly more effective.
🌬️ Breathing Pattern
Going Down
Exhale on each rotation
At Bottom
Inhale between
Coming Up

⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Going too fast — loses most muscle activation
✗Pulling on the neck
✗Not fully extending the leg
✗Lifting lower back off floor
✅ Pro Tips
→Slow tempo (3 seconds per side) dramatically increases intensity
→Keep opposite shoulder off floor during rotation
→Exhale on each crunch and rotation for maximum activation
