Pull-Up

The ultimate test of relative strength. The most impressive bodyweight movement you can master.

Latissimus DorsiBicepsRhomboidsRear DeltoidsCore
Category Pull
Difficulty 🟡 Intermediate
Primary Latissimus Dorsi, Biceps
Sets · Reps 3 · 1–5
📋 Step-by-Step Form
1
Dead Hang
Overhand grip slightly wider than shoulders. Arms fully extended. Relax shoulders completely.
2
Initiate with Scapula
BEFORE bending arms — depress and retract shoulder blades. Think: shoulder blades into back pockets.
3
Drive Elbows to Hips
Pull by thinking 'drive elbows toward hip pockets.' This naturally activates the lats.
4
Chin Over Bar
Pull until chin clears the bar. Aim for chest-to-bar for maximum range of motion.
5
Slow Descent
Lower in 3–4 seconds back to full dead hang. Do NOT drop fast — this is where gains happen.
🌬️ Breathing Pattern
Going Down
Inhale at hang
At Bottom
Exhale pulling up
Coming Up
Inhale lowering

⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗Kipping/swinging — build strict strength first
✗Not returning to dead hang — partial reps
✗Shrugging shoulders — depress scapula
✗Chin tilting to reach bar — use actual strength
✅ Pro Tips
→Squeeze bar as hard as possible to activate more muscles
→Cross feet to prevent swinging
→'Bend the bar apart' for external shoulder rotation
→Negatives (slow lowering) build strength fastest
